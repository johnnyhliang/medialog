 1. Controlled ankle rolls:
    - Sit on a chair with your foot off the ground
    - Slowly roll your ankle inward (inversion) and then outward (eversion)
    - Gradually increase the range of motion as you get stronger
2. Resistance band inversion/eversion:
    - Sit on the floor with your leg extended
    - Loop a resistance band around your foot
    - Slowly turn your foot inward against the resistance, then outward
    - Focus on controlled movements through the full range of motion
3. Single-leg balance on unstable surface:
    - Stand on one foot on a wobble board, foam pad, or folded towel
    - Try to maintain balance for 30 seconds to 1 minute
    - This challenges your ankle stability in multiple directions
4. Standing ankle tilts:
    - Stand with your feet hip-width apart
    - Slowly lift the outer edges of your feet, putting weight on the inner edges
    - Then reverse, lifting the inner edges and putting weight on the outer edges
    - Hold each position for a few seconds
5. Proprioception drills:
    - Stand on one foot and have someone gently push you in different directions
    - Try to maintain your balance while resisting the pushes
    - This improves your ankle's ability to react quickly to unexpected movements
6. Ankle hops:
    - Hop side to side on one foot, landing on the outer edge of your foot
    - Progress to hopping in different directions (forward, backward, diagonally)
7. Tilt board exercises:
    - Use a tilt board or make one by attaching a round object under a flat board
    - Stand on the board and tilt it in different directions, controlling the movement with your ankle  



- Ankle eversion/inversion with resistance band:
    - Sit on the floor with your leg extended
    - Loop a resistance band around your foot
    - Slowly turn your foot inward and outward against the resistance
- Calf raises:
    - Stand on the edge of a step
    - Raise up on your toes, then slowly lower back down
    - For added difficulty, do this on one foot at a time
- Ankle dorsiflexion with resistance band:
    - Sit on the floor with your leg extended
    - Loop a resistance band around your foot
    - Pull your toes towards your shin against the resistance
- Single-leg balance exercises:
    - Stand on one foot
    - Maintain balance for 30 seconds to 1 minute
    - Progress to doing this with your eyes closed or on an unstable surface
