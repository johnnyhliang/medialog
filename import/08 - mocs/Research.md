You need the following books:

1.      Designing Experiments and Analyzing Data: A Model Comparison Perspective by Scott E. Maxwell, Harold D. Delaney & Ken Kelley

This bookprovides a comprehensive approach to experimental design, emphasizing the importance of model comparison.

2.      Experimental Design: Procedures for the Behavioral Sciences by Roger E. Kirk

This one focuses on design principles and statistical analysis relevant to behavioral sciences, applicable to neurocomputing studies.

3.      Writing for Computer Science by Justin Zobel

This book offers practical advice on writing technical documents, focusing on structure, clarity, and flow, tailored for computer science.

4.      The Elements of Style by William Strunk Jr. and E.B. White

This offers a classic guide to writing that emphasizes clarity and conciseness, beneficial for any scientific writing.

5.      Creating More Effective Graphs by Naomi B. Robbins

This book provides a practical guide to creating clear and effective graphs, covering color choices, sizes, and fonts.

6.      Data Visualization: A Practical Introduction by Kieran Healy

This one provides principles and guidelines for visualizing data effectively, with a focus on clarity and aesthetics.

7.      The Scientist's Guide to Writing by Stephen B. Heard

This book focuses on writing as a skill, providing practical advice for scientists to communicate their research effectively.
